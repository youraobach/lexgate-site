Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 7, 2026

LexGate is built around a simple principle: your data is yours, and it never leaves your device. This policy explains what the app does — and, mostly, what it doesn't do.

What LexGate does on your device

LexGate helps you limit access to apps you choose and learn English vocabulary in the process. To do this, the app stores the following information locally on your iPhone only: the list of apps you selected for blocking (as anonymous tokens provided by Apple's Screen Time technology), your learning progress (words shown, answers, review schedule), your chosen language level, and the name you enter during onboarding.

What we collect

Nothing. LexGate has no user accounts, no sign-in, no backend servers, no analytics SDKs, no advertising frameworks, and no crash-reporting tools that transmit data. The app is fully functional offline. We — the developers — have no way of knowing which apps you block, which words you learn, or whether you use LexGate at all.

Screen Time technology

App blocking is powered by Apple's Screen Time frameworks (Family Controls, Managed Settings, and Device Activity). LexGate requests Screen Time authorization for your own device and your own use — it is a self-management tool, not a parental-control service. Apple's frameworks are designed so that apps like LexGate receive only opaque tokens, not the actual identities of the apps you select. You can revoke this authorization at any time in Settings → Screen Time.

Notifications

LexGate asks for notification permission for one purpose: when you tap a blocked app, a notification is the bridge that opens the exercise. Notifications are generated locally on your device; there is no remote push infrastructure.

Children

LexGate is intended for adults managing their own screen time and is not directed at children.

Data deletion

Because everything is stored locally, deleting the app deletes all of its data. There is nothing to request from us, because we hold nothing.

Changes to this policy

If a future version of LexGate ever changes how data is handled — for example, by adding optional iCloud sync — this policy will be updated before that version is released, and the change will be clearly described in the app.
